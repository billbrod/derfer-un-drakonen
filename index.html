Villages and Dragons:

A Yiddish Dungeons and Dragons Project

About the project

Below is a variety of Yiddish words to help you make characters, play, and run a Yiddish D&D game. These were created in conjunction with the Yiddish D&D character sheet. If you have any suggested modifications or new words, please open an issue on the GitHub repo. I am happy to add words or fix any errors I have made. Not all words I am happy with, and there are likely better words out there (though maybe the ones I have chosen are fine). The yellow rows mark words that I am not 100% happy with, while the bold text marks words I created.

About me

My name is Marnie, I am an avid Yiddishist and roleplaying game enthusiast. I’ve been studying Yiddish for 2 ½ years. I love old jewish paper cut art and painted synagogues. I hope a mix of all these interests has come together to make something other people can enjoy.

This project would not be possible without a few people

First and foremost this project was inspired by three images on Yugntruf-Youth for Yiddish facebook from Yiddish Vokh. These images have a small vocab list, an English D&D character sheet filled out in Yiddish, and a group of people playing D&D out on a picnic bench. Since then I have not been able to get this idea out of my head, so thanks for the inspiration!

Second, the font used in this project is Hadasim CLM from the Culmus Project. I love the fonts on there, please check them out!

~~thank Billy for website~~

Last but not least I wanted to thank my friend Seth Weisberg for the help and for checking over some of my work. I would not have had the confidence to finish this project without his help.

Have fun role playing and let me know if you would like a matching spell sheet.
